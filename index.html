About Kirkland

Kirkland, Washington is a vibrant lakeside community east of Seattle. Known for its waterfront parks, public art, tech presence, and small-business culture, Kirkland blends natural beauty with strong community values.

History of Kirkland

1886

Kirkland was founded by Peter Kirk with plans for a steel industry on Lake Washington.

1905

Ferry access connected Kirkland to Seattle, boosting growth.

1980s

Tech companies began to take root, driving rapid expansion.

2011

Annexation of surrounding neighborhoods increased population and area.

Today

Kirkland is home to diverse communities, businesses, waterfront recreation, and strong neighborhood connections.

Discover Parks

Also known as Marina Park, Marina Park features a sandy beach, boat launch, public art and a beautiful open-air pavilion as well as summer concerts and magnificent views. "A perfect lakeside retreat for families and nature lovers." 25 Lakeshore Plaza Drive, Kirkland 98033

Juanita Bay Park abounds with flora and fauna and features views of Forbes Creek Wetland, Juanita Beach and Juanita Bay. Juanita Bay Park is a Green Kirkland Partnership restoration area. "Ideal for wildlife viewing and peaceful walks." 2201 Market St, Kirkland 98033

McAuliffe Park features grassy lawns, ornamental plants, a playground, and a community garden. "Community hub with playgrounds and sports fields." 10824 NE 116 Street, Kirkland 98034

Doris Cooper Houghton Beach Park is a waterfront community park located in Kirkland's Lakeview neighborhood that features a playground, picnic areas, sand volleyball court, and seasonal swimming area. "Small beach with a charming view for picnics and kayaking." 5811 Lake Washington Blvd, Kirkland 98033

Everest Park is a community park with many amenities, including pickleball courts, ballfields, a playground, and a picnic shelter. "Quiet neighborhood park with open green spaces." 500 8th St S, Kirkland 98033
