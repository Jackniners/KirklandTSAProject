About Kirkland

Kirkland is a suburb of Seattle on the east side of Lake Washington. It is well-known for its parks, trails, walkable waterfront downtown, and significant tech presence. There is around 100,000 residents and excellent schools. EvergreenHealth is one of the major employers, and the name "Kirkland" is well-known because the city inspired the original name of Costco's Kirkland Signature brand. It provides great restaurants, fantastic views, outdoor activities, and a vibrant sense of community.

Why Move to Kirkland?

Kirkland is a beautiful and welcoming lakeside city known for its safe neighborhoods, thriving small businesses, and active community life. Here's why many people choose to move here:

  • Clean, safe neighborhoods with strong community feel
  • Waterfront parks, beaches, and scenic walking paths
  • Highly rated schools and youth programs
  • Great restaurants, coffee shops, and local businesses
  • Short commute to Seattle, Bellevue, or Redmond
  • Year-round festivals, markets, and events

History of Kirkland

1886

Kirkland was founded by Peter Kirk with plans for a steel industry on Lake Washington.

1905

Ferry access connected Kirkland to Seattle, boosting growth.

1980

Tech companies began to take root, driving rapid expansion.

2011

Annexation of surrounding neighborhoods increased population and area.

Today

Kirkland is home to diverse communities, businesses, waterfront recreation, and strong neighborhood connections.
